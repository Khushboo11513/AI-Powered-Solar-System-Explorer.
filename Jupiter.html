♃ Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, is a gas giant with a diameter of about 139,820 kilometers (86,881 miles). It has a massive atmosphere made mostly of hydrogen and helium, and its iconic Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging for centuries.

Key Facts about Jupiter:

  • 🌌 Largest planet: Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, with a diameter about 11 times that of Earth.
  • 🌀 The Great Red Spot: Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has lasted for at least 400 years.
  • 🌑 Many moons: Jupiter has at least 79 moons, including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
  • 🌡️ Cold atmosphere: Despite its size, Jupiter’s upper atmosphere is extremely cold, with temperatures around -145°C (-234°F).
  • 🏃 Fast rotation: A day on Jupiter lasts only 10 hours, the shortest of any planet in the solar system.
  • 🌠 Powerful magnetic field: Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet, 14 times stronger than Earth’s.
