Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, is a gas giant known for its magnificent and expansive ring system. This massive planet is a wonder of the solar system, with unique features that make it an extraordinary celestial body.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: Saturn is the second-largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter about 9 times that of Earth.
  • Composition: Saturn is mostly composed of hydrogen and helium.
  • Rings: Its iconic rings are made of ice and rock particles.
  • Moons: Saturn has 83 confirmed moons, including Titan, which is larger than Mercury.

Orbital Characteristics:

  • Distance from the Sun: About 9.5 AU.
  • Orbital Period: 29.4 Earth years.
  • Rotation Period: 10.7 hours.

Key Points:

  • Least Dense Planet: Saturn is the least dense planet in the solar system.
  • Giant Storm: Saturn's Great White Spot appears every 29 Earth years.
